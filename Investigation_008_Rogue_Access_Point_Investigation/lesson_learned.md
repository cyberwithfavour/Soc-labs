---

## What I Learned

This investigation taught me that not every threat comes from outside the organization. Unauthorized wireless devices can also pose serious security risks.

I learned how a **Rogue Access Point** can imitate a legitimate corporate Wi-Fi network and trick users into connecting, potentially exposing sensitive information or user credentials.

---

## Key Takeaway

Wireless monitoring is an important part of network security. Detecting unauthorized access points early helps prevent attacks such as credential theft, Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) attacks, and unauthorized network access.

---

## Personal Reflection

Before this investigation, I thought wireless security was mostly about using strong Wi-Fi passwords. Now I understand that SOC analysts also monitor wireless networks for rogue devices and suspicious activity to protect both users and the organization's infrastructure.
